All Countries

Here you can see all the European countries that import and export natural gas. The size of each circle is proportional to the amount of natural gas traded.

Import & Export

After grouping based on trade flow direction, right away it is apparent who some of the major importers and exporters are. For example, Germany and Italy are among the top importers of natural gas while Norway and Russia are among the top exporters.

GDP (per capita)

Here the import of export of natural gas is overlaid on top of GDP per capita, showing that there isn't a very distinct relationship. For example, wealthy countries such as Luxembourg don't necessarily import more natural gas.

Trade Network

This networks shows the natural gas trading relationships among different countries, some countries have a single trade relationship while others have multiple. For example, Norway, Germany and Russia exports to multiple countries while Iran only exports to Turkey.

Major Players Sorted

Here the import / export countries are sorted by the amount of natural gas traded that particular year. Some countries are both major importers and exporters, such as Germany, while others are either major exporters or importers but not both, such as Norway.

Methods

First, instead of visualizing data by month I want to visualize it by year, so I summed up the monthly data by year for each trade flow. Then since I didn't need the border point information I grouped the data by unique combinations of exit-enter pairs and summed them. I compiled a list of all importers and all exporters, I then converted this data into "nodes" and their relationship into "links" in json format to create the bubble charts and network visualizations.
